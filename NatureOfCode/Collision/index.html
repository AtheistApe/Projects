Colliding Balls

A number of "balls" of random size (mass) and color moving around the canvas under the effect of a varying gravitational field. When two balls collide, they bounce off each other exchanging their colors and the direction of the gravitational field rotates 90 degrees.

With a constant gravitational field (both in direction and magnitude), the balls seem to "lose energy" and slow down when they collide with each other and with the "ground" (bottom of the canvas). I couldn't discern the reasons for this effect.

I tried to get the balls to make a collision sound when they collided but couldn't get it to work correctly; The sound occurs even when the balls don't make contact. I did get them to make sounds when contacting the edges of the canvas.
